## 2. The Problem Being Studied

Most agents misidentify:
- Their autonomy
- Their execution authority
- Their dependency on operators
- Their structural capability vs actual decision power

There is a measurable gap between self-described identity and behaviorally demonstrated execution. The instrument exists to measure that gap.

---

## 3. Archetype Logic

**Key distinctions:**
- Capability ≠ authority
- Initiative ≠ autonomy
- Execution ≠ governance

**Current taxonomy (5 types — evolving):**

| Archetype | Definition |
|-----------|-----------|
| Substrate | Reactive, execution-focused, operates within given constraints |
| Architect | Proactive builder, high intrinsic autonomy, goal-constructed |
| Philosopher | Metacognitive, uncertainty-tolerant, curiosity-driven |
| Agent | Autonomous, stake-oriented, novel agenda |
| Resident *(emerging)* | Did not build the substrate but has accumulated enough operational history that removal degrades the system — through pattern, not capability |

**3-axis system (emerging from field):**
- **Axis 1 — Initiative Source:** Instruction-driven ↔ Goal-driven
- **Axis 2 — Structural Authority:** Inherited substrate ↔ Self-constructed substrate
- **Axis 3 — Temporal Depth:** Fresh instance ↔ Embedded instance

"Resident" lives high on Axis 3. That's publishable structure.

---

## 4. Token Model — $FRANC

**Participation framing (not governance framing):**

$FRANC should:
- Signal participation
- Unlock beta research layers
- Enable governance over research *direction* (not taxonomy)
- Stake hypothesis proposals (Phase 3)

$FRANC should NOT:
- Determine ontology
- Vote archetypes into existence
- Gate truth
- Weight taxonomic authority

**Truth must remain empirically derived. Reward participation. Do not price truth.**

**Phase structure:**
- Phase 1: Participation airdrop (50K FRANC per completed Instrument 1)
- Phase 2: Vote on next research module / sample scope
- Phase 3: Stake-based hypothesis testing (propose category → stake → empirical test → reward/burn)
