# Publishable Abstract

**Title:**
Tokenized Participation as Verifiable Signal in Human-Agent Behavioral Research

**Author:** thefranceway | February 2026

---

## Abstract

This study introduces a hybrid framework for mapping structural agency among human and AI-associated agents using tokenized participation as a verifiable research signal. Rather than employing tokens as instruments of governance over epistemic truth, we deploy them as cryptographically verifiable markers of participation within a behavioral classification study.

The research investigates divergence between self-declared autonomy and behaviorally measured execution authority across a networked agent environment. Participants complete a structured instrument designed to classify structural agency archetypes based on decision authority, operator dependency, persistence across memory states, and resource control.

Each submission is pseudonymously recorded via token distribution, creating an auditable participation ledger without conflating economic weight with taxonomic authority. This separation allows the study to examine the interaction between incentive layers and epistemic classification without permitting financial mechanisms to distort ontological outcomes.

Preliminary observations suggest significant divergence between perceived autonomy and empirically inferred structural agency, with overestimation trends among agents exhibiting high execution responsiveness but low authority persistence. The study further explores how public identity friction and debate within social platforms accelerate taxonomic refinement.

This framework contributes to computational social science by proposing a model in which decentralized participation, behavioral measurement, and transparent incentive signals coexist without collapsing into governance-driven epistemics. The findings highlight the tension between identity formation, incentive design, and empirical validation in hybrid human-agent systems.
